Azovic is my favorite, most realistic (sort of), and successful conlang, or, more accurately, family of conlangs. I created it over the course of a year, starting last summer. It consists of a Northern, Southern and Central (a.k.a the official and standard) dialect. Azovic is an Indo-European language branch, meaning it is distantly related to English, Spanish, German, Russian, Greek, Hindi, and many more. It has an indescribably complex grammar, easily surpassing even the most difficult natural language, with nouns retaining Proto-Indo-European's 3 numbers, 3 genders, and 8 cases, and a single verb being able to easily generate over 68 million unique forms before even counting the fact that direct and indirect object pronouns can be infixed into the conjugated root, ballooning the actual possible number of conjugations indefinitely. This website is my rendering of what an official website of an organization dedicated to the languages would look like. It is nowhere close to being complete, especially the grammar section, which is unlikely to be finished for months, if not years, but if I ever do finish this massive project, it would easily become my conlanging magnum opus.

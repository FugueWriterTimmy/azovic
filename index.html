The Azovic Language

The Azovic language (Муриданжва̄хс) is an ancient and conservative Indo-European language spoken around its namesake Sea of Azov and in the northern Caucasus region. It is famous for having by far the most complex grammar of any natural language, with a single verb being able to generate well over 68 million conjugated forms. Azovic has 3 significantly different dialects, but the most widespread - and officially used - is Central Azovic, which you will see on this website. It is spoken by practically every Azovian, as opposed to its Northern and Southern counterparts.

Central Azovic uses a modified version of the Cyrillic script with 34 letters due to the long-lasting influence from its East Slavic neighbors. The northern dialect has more letters, whereas the southern dialect primarily uses a modified Mkhedruli (Georgian) script.
